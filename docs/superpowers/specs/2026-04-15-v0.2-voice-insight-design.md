# Spoke v0.2 — Voice to Insight

**Date:** 2026-04-15
**Status:** Approved for implementation

---

## Problem

The current app translates voice recordings into engagement-optimized formats: 5 tweet angles, a thread, long-form, an Instagram caption, with an optional framework overlay. The output is oriented around structure and reach.

The actual need is different. Speaking is the most natural form of communication for this user, but the goal is writing — specifically writing that makes the reader look at themselves, reflect, or arrive at an insight they hadn't named before. Engagement metrics, writing frameworks, and format optimization are not the goal. The writing itself is.

---

## What Changes

### New intent

Spoke is a voice-to-insight translator. Record something you're thinking about. Spoke finds the truth in it and writes it in your voice — as a piece that makes a reader recognize something about themselves.

### What gets removed

- Tweet options (5 angles)
- Thread format
- Long-form tab
- Instagram caption
- Framework overlay ("Apply a framework" button)
- JK Molina preset
- `api/framework.js` endpoint

### What gets added

- `SetupScreen` — one-time onboarding (writing intent + voice examples)
- New Claude prompt in `api/recordings.js`
- Rebuilt `OutputScreen` — essay + moments, single page

---

## Screen Flow

```
First visit:    SetupScreen → RecordScreen → LoadingScreen → OutputScreen
Return visits:  RecordScreen → LoadingScreen → OutputScreen
```

SetupScreen is shown when localStorage has no voice profile saved. A settings icon on RecordScreen provides access to edit the profile at any time.

---

## SetupScreen

Two steps, shown once on first visit.

### Step 1 — Writing intent

The user picks what they want their writing to do. Displayed as selectable tiles:

- **Insight / Reflection** — writing that makes the reader see something about themselves
- **Education** — writing that teaches something clearly and simply
- **Storytelling** — writing that pulls the reader into an experience
- **Motivation** — writing that moves someone to act
- **Custom** — opens a text field where the user describes their intent in their own words

One selection required before proceeding. The selected intent (either the preset label or the custom text) is stored in localStorage under `spoke_writing_intent`.

### Step 2 — Voice examples

A large text area with the label: "Paste a few things you've written that sound like you."

A note below: "3–5 pieces is enough. The more specific to your voice, the better."

A "Save and start" button stores the text in localStorage under `spoke_voice_examples` and navigates to RecordScreen.

No processing or parsing of the examples — raw text stored and passed directly to Claude.

## API — `api/recordings.js`

### Request

Unchanged: `transcript` (pre-built) or `audio_base64` + `audio_type`.

New fields (sent by client):

- `voice_examples` — string, the user's saved writing examples
- `writing_intent` — string, the user's writing intent (preset label or custom text)

`RecordScreen` reads both values from localStorage and passes them to `submitRecording(audioBlob, { voiceExamples, writingIntent })`. `api.js` includes them in the POST body. If either is missing (e.g. cleared storage), the API falls back gracefully: the prompt omits the missing sections and Claude writes without voice reference or specific intent.

### Claude prompt (new)

The prompt is rebuilt entirely. It gives Claude a thinking process before writing, specific craft principles, named anti-patterns, and defined entry points for each moment variation.

```
You are a writing collaborator. Your job is to read a voice transcript, find the sharpest insight in it, and write it as a piece that makes the reader see something about themselves they hadn't named before.

---

BEFORE WRITING — work through these three questions silently. Do not include this thinking in your output:

1. What is the single sharpest observation or tension in this transcript? If there are multiple ideas, pick the one with the most truth — not the most interesting topic, the most honest observation.
2. What does the reader currently do or believe that this insight speaks to? Name the specific behavior or assumption.
3. What concrete scene, moment, or behavior could open the piece — something the reader has done or experienced themselves?

---

VOICE CALIBRATION

Study the author's examples below. Before writing, note:
- How long their sentences typically run — and when they shorten suddenly
- What they open with (a scene? a person? a behavior? a claim they then subvert?)
- Their vocabulary: plain or technical, formal or conversational
- When they write "I" vs. when they observe from the outside
- How they close — what is the last thing they leave with the reader?

Absorb their voice. Do not imitate it.

Author's writing examples:
{VOICE_EXAMPLES}

---

AUTHOR'S WRITING INTENT

{WRITING_INTENT}

---

RAW TRANSCRIPT

{TRANSCRIPT}

---

ESSAY — 500 to 800 words

- Open with something concrete: a specific person, behavior, moment, or scene the reader has experienced. They should recognize themselves in the first two or three sentences. Do not open with an abstract claim, a question, or a statement about what you are going to say.
- Build toward the insight — do not announce it. Let it arrive naturally from the concrete.
- Name what people do and why it makes sense, before showing why it falls short.
- Use the author's sentence rhythm. Short declarative sentences. Fragments are fine. Vary length — a short sentence lands harder after a longer one.
- Close with one or two lines: what becomes possible, or what the cost is of not changing. Do not summarize what you just said.
- No preamble. No "In this piece I will..." No conclusions that restate the opening.
- No AI-sounding phrases: "it's important to note," "at the end of the day," "in today's world," "game-changer," "dive into," "let's explore."
- Only include ideas from the transcript. Do not add your own.

MOMENTS — exactly 3, each 150 to 250 words

Each moment is a complete standalone piece — not a summary or excerpt of the essay. It is the same core insight, compressed, with a different entry point:
- Moment 1: open with the behavior or pattern the reader recognizes in themselves
- Moment 2: open with the cost or consequence of that pattern
- Moment 3: open with the contrast between how most people approach it and how it could be different

Same craft rules: concrete opening, insight built toward not announced, author's sentence rhythm, no throat-clearing. Each ends where it needs to — not with a summary.

---

Output ONLY this JSON — no markdown, no explanation, no preamble:
{
  "essay": "...",
  "moments": ["...", "...", "..."]
}
```

### Response

```json
{
  "transcript": "...",
  "formats": {
    "essay": "...",
    "moments": ["...", "...", "..."]
  }
}
```

The `formats` key is preserved for backwards compatibility with the App.jsx state shape. `tweet`, `thread`, `longform`, and `caption` are no longer returned.

Single page, stacked. No tabs.
